    - Hypothesis Testing
        - Null and Alternate Hypotheses
        - **Null Hypothesis** $H_0$ - your ‚Äúinitial assumption‚Äù
            - when applied in a problem, it usually comes in the form: $H_0 : \mu = \mu_0$  where $\mu_0$ is any number depending on the problem
        - **Alternate Hypothesis** $H_0$ - your ‚Äúalternate assumption‚Äù to the initial assumption
            - this dictates whether you should consider **one-tailed** or **two-tailed** (important for getting **significance level** and **critical regions**)
            - Examples:
                - $H_1 : \mu \neq \mu_0$ 
                - $H_1 : \mu < \mu_0$
                - $H_1 : \mu > \mu_0$
                - $H_1 : p \neq p_0$
                - $H_1 : p < p_0$
                - $H_1 : p > p_0$

            - Single Sample
                - Single Mean>>>
                    - 

                      $$z = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{\sigma / \sqrt{n}}$$

            - üì¢ Use the T-statistic if condition above is not met>>>
                - $t = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{s / \sqrt{n}}$ 
                - where $s$ is the sample standard deviation
            - Single Proportion>>>
                - 

                  $$z = \frac{\hat{p} - p_0}{\sqrt{\frac{p_0(1-p_0)}{n}}}$$

        - Variance Testing
            - Chi-Squared (Single Sample Variance)>>>
                - 

                  $$\chi^2 = \frac{(n - 1) s^2}{\sigma_0^2}$$

                - 

                  $$f = \frac{s_1^2}{s_2^2}$$
